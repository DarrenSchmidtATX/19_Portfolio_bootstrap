Case Study: Website Redesign for Greyhound Rescue Austin

My Role: Lead UX Researcher Co-UX Designer

The Challenge

Greyhound Rescue Austin (GRA) is dedicated to rescuing, rehabilitating and re-homing Greyhounds in Austin, TX. Our goal was to redesign the GRA website around users’ primary needs of adopting, fostering, volunteering.

Design

As we wrapped up our research and analysis, we went into the design and iteration phase where we brainstormed our design based on targeted users’ needs, goals, and frustrations from the GRA website. Then, we began our design based on a specific user flow and implemented these into XD wireframes and responsive web prototypes through several iterations.

LOW-FI WIREFRAME OVERVIEW

Testing/Feedback

From our prototype user testing, data suggested that users wanted to know more about each greyhound dog. So, we added a section with biographical success stories to inform users about each dog. Our adoption page also includes a filtered search with tags to help users’ find the right dog.

Outcomes

We redesigned the GRA website around users’ primary needs of adopting greyhound dogs. Our redesign modernizes the website, and helps this non-profit organization accomplish their mission: Rescue Greyhounds.
